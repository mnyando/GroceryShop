In Kenyan cooking, tomatoes are absolutely indispensable. They are the soul of kachumbari — our beloved fresh salsa of raw tomatoes, onions, and dhania (coriander) that accompanies nyama choma and grilled fish. They also form the flavourful base of nyanya mchuzi (tomato stew) that goes with ugali, rice, or chapati. Freshness matters: a ripe, farm-fresh tomato gives a depth of flavour that no processed alternative can match.

Sautéed with onions and a pinch of salt, it pairs beautifully with ugali for a wholesome, budget-friendly meal. It is equally delicious alongside githeri (maize and beans) or tucked into a chapati wrap for a quick, nutritious lunch.
